Our Story

From Fish Farm to the Only High-Altitude Alligator Farm in the World

Colorado Gators started as a humble fish farm in the San Luis Valley, and like many rural ventures, it evolved in the most unexpected way. What began as a way to supply bait fish slowly attracted a very different kind of animal — alligators.

Those gators were originally "convenient garbage disposals," as the family jokes. But over the years, they grew from a handful of surplus reptiles into a thriving community of hundreds of rescued animals — and a one-of-a-kind roadside attraction that draws visitors from across the country.

Today, Colorado Gators is a registered reptile rescue and education center, a San Luis Valley landmark, and a favorite stop for road-trippers exploring Highway 160.

More Than an Attraction — A Rescue

Every animal at Colorado Gators has a story. Many were surrendered by owners who could no longer care for them. Some were rescued from neglect or dangerous situations. All of them receive daily care from the family who runs this place with genuine love.

The collection includes American alligators, crocodiles, caimans, tortoises of various species, turtles, snakes, and more — each with its own personality and history. Some residents have lived here for decades.

The Morris family has opened their doors to the public not for profit, but to share their passion and to educate visitors about these remarkable creatures. Roadside America has featured them as a must-stop roadside attraction, and word-of-mouth keeps families coming back.

Legends

Meet Morris — The Alligator Who Was in Every Great Film

Before he retired to the Colorado mountains, Morris lived a life most alligators only dream of: a Hollywood career spanning over 30 years.

Morris was no ordinary alligator. At 640 pounds of pure, prehistoric charisma, he appeared in ALLIGATOR (1980) — the cult classic about a mutated sewer gator — and went on to star in dozens of films and TV shows. Movies like Happy Gilmore, Hot Shots!, Top Gun, and countless others featured Morris in everything from background cameos to memorable close-ups.

From 1975 to 2006, Morris was Hollywood's go-to alligator. He was the original screen gator, the actor's actor, the one you called when you needed a real deal in the scene. He worked until his retirement, when he found a forever home right here in the San Luis Valley.

Morris lived to be over 80 years old — an impressive lifespan for an alligator — and spent his golden years basking in the high-altitude Colorado sun, watched over by the family who loved him. He was a legend on screen, and a legend off it.

Elevation: Approximately 9,400 feet. Dress in layers — mountain weather changes fast, even in summer!
