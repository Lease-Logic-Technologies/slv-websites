The Story

From Fish Farm
to Roadside Legend

In the high desert of Colorado's San Luis Valley, something unexpected took root. What began as a humble fish farm in the 1970s became home to one of the most unusual wildlife collections in the American West.

The gators started as a practical solution — convenient garbage disposals for the operation. But as the collection grew, so did curiosity. Visitors driving through the remote mountain valley couldn't believe their eyes: alligators, crocodiles, and tortoises living at nearly 10,000 feet above sea level.

What started as an act of necessity evolved into Colorado Gators Reptile Park — a family-run reptile rescue and tourist attraction, now featured on Roadside America as one of the most unique stops on the American road trip circuit.

Experiences

Get Up Close.
Nothing From the Railing.

This isn't a zoo where you watch through glass. At Colorado Gators, you touch, feed, and hold some of the most extraordinary reptiles on Earth.

Hold an Alligator

Get your hands on a live American alligator. Staff guide you through safe handling techniques. A once-in-a-lifetime experience for thrill-seekers and families alike.

Feed the Gators

Watch hundreds of alligators erupt from their pools as you toss food. The spectacle is raw, primal, and unforgettable. A daily highlight for every visitor.

Fish for Carp

Catch your own carp from our ponds to feed the alligators. It's part of the experience — and part of how the farm has operated for generations.

Pet a Tortoise

Meet the gentle giants of the farm. Our tortoises are approachable and friendly — you can touch and feed them up close. Kids love this one.

Rescue Stories

Every animal here has a story. Many were surrendered, rescued, or relocated. Learn about the rehabilitation efforts that make Colorado Gators a conservation partner.

Educational Tours

From reptile biology to ecosystem ecology, our staff shares deep knowledge about the animals. School groups and curious travelers welcome.

The Animals

Hundreds of Rescued
Reptiles. One Remarkable Collection.

Colorado Gators houses an extraordinary variety of reptiles rescued from situations across North America and beyond. Every species here has a story of survival.

Reptile

American Alligator

The cornerstone of the collection. Multiple adults and juveniles roam the facilities year-round.

Reptile

Saltwater Crocodile

One of the largest living reptile species on Earth, housed at nearly 10,000 feet elevation.

Reptile

Caiman

Smaller relatives of the alligator, including several species from Central and South America.

Rare Specimen

Albino Alligators

Extremely rare and visually striking. The white-pink coloration makes them a collection highlight.

Reptile

Tortoises

Various species of land tortoises, friendly and approachable. A favorite for hands-on interaction.

Reptile

Turtles

Water and land turtles from various habitats, some rescued and rehabilitated at the park.

Reptile

Snakes

Multiple species including pythons and other non-venomous snakes from around the world.

Reptile

Pythons

Large constrictors that add to the diversity of the collection and fascination of visitors.

In Memoriam

MORRIS

1943 — 2023  |  Age 80+

"Morris wasn't just an alligator. He was a Hollywood legend — he appeared in the movie Alligator (1980), Happy Gilmore, and dozens of television shows and films from 1975 to 2006. He lived a remarkable life, and in retirement, he found peace here in Colorado."

Morris lived at Colorado Gators in his later years, a true icon who bridged two worlds — the wild nature of alligators and the bright lights of Hollywood. He passed away at the age of 80+, an extraordinary lifespan for an American alligator. He is remembered fondly by staff, visitors, and fans who saw him on screen decades ago.
