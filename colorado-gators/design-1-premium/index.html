Our Story

From Fish Farm
to Reptile Rescue

Colorado Gators began as a humble fish farm in the high desert of the San Luis Valley. When the owners needed an efficient way to dispose of waste from the operation, they turned to the most practical solution available — a few alligators. What started as a functional arrangement grew into something far more remarkable.

Today, Colorado Gators is the only high-altitude alligator farm in the world, home to hundreds of rescued reptiles including alligators, crocodiles, caimans, tortoises, and turtles. It has become a beloved classic roadside attraction, featured on Roadside America and visited by thousands of travelers each year who come to experience something truly unique at 9,400 feet above sea level.

Our Legacy

“

Morris

640 lbs · Hollywood Legend · 1975–2006

Morris wasn't just an alligator. He was an actor. Appearing in dozens of films and television shows from 1975 to 2006, Morris brought prehistoric charm to Hollywood screens. His most famous roles include the title creature in Alligator (1980) and a memorable appearance in Adam Sandler's Happy Gilmore — where he literally stole the scene.

After a long career in entertainment, Morris retired to Colorado Gators, where he spent his golden years at 9,400 feet. He lived a peaceful retirement surrounded by his reptile family, far from the chaos of film sets. Morris passed away at the remarkable age of 80+, having earned his rest in the quiet Colorado mountains he came to love.
