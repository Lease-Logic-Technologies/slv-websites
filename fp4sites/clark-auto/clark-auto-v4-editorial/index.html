The beginning

In 2003, Ron Clark opened the doors of Clark Auto Service on Tremont Street in Alamosa. With decades of experience working on classic cars — particularly pre-1950s vehicles — Ron built a reputation for honest work and rare mechanical talent that few could match.

What started as a one-man operation in a small shop has grown into a full-service repair facility. But the core values remain the same: do the work right, be fair with the customer, and never sell a repair that isn't needed.

Two generations

Today, Chris Clark — Ron's son — runs day-to-day operations. While Ron specializes in vintage and classic vehicles, Chris brings modern expertise in engine diagnostics, hybrid systems, and advanced electrical troubleshooting.

"My dad taught me that every car tells a story. You just have to know how to listen. Now I get to combine that with tools he could only dream about 20 years ago."

Together, the Clark family handles everything from a 1940 Ford's carburetor to a 2024 Toyota's hybrid battery. That's the kind of range you find at very few independent shops.

Our philosophy

We don't use high-pressure sales tactics. We don't recommend unnecessary repairs. When you bring your vehicle to Clark Auto Service, you get a thorough diagnosis, an honest assessment, and a clear quote before we touch a wrench.

We've been part of this community for over 20 years. Our reputation is built on the cars we fix and the relationships we build. That matters more to us than any award or certificate.
