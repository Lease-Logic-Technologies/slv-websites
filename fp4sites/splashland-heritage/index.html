Our Heritage

Splashland Hot Springs Swimming Pool began in 1955 when the founders were drilling for oil on the site and struck an unexpected geothermal pocket of water — flowing at 104\°F straight from the earth beneath the San Luis Valley.

What could have been a geological curiosity became something enduring. They turned a drilling accident into a family-run swimming pool that has served Alamosa, the entire San Luis Valley, and visitors from across the country and around the world ever since.

"The water enters the pool at 104\°F year-round with average temperatures ranging 88–96\°F pool wide." — Splashland Hot Springs

Today, Splashland remains a community institution. It is where families come for their first swim lessons. Where retirees come for morning laps. Where out-of-state visitors discover that Colorado has something most people don't know about: a natural hot spring swimming pool.
