Our Story

One Well. 70+ Years.

In 1955, two men were drilling for oil in the San Luis Valley and struck something unexpected: natural geothermal water flowing at 104\°F straight from the earth.

What could have been abandoned became Splashland — a family hot spring pool that has served Alamosa, the San Luis Valley, and visitors from out of state and overseas for more than seven decades.

Today, Splashland continues that legacy. The water still flows hot. The community that built it still stands. And the pool that was an accident of geology has become a destination.
