120 Years of Service

Rotary was founded on February 23, 1905, when Paul Harris, a young attorney, blew into Chicago on the first transcontinental railroad. Missing the camaraderie of his days in Salt Lake City, he sat down with three other businessmen and formed what became Rotary International.

Today, Rotary is the oldest and largest service club in the world — 1.2 million members across more than 200 countries, united by a shared commitment to service above self. The Alamosa Rotary Club carries that tradition forward right here in the San Luis Valley.

1905

Paul Harris founds Rotary in Chicago with three fellow businessmen

1917

The Four-Way Test is introduced as a moral code for Rotarians

1945

Rotary becomes a member of the UN Economic and Social Council

1979

Rotary joins the global fight to eradicate polio

Today

1.2M+ members serving in 200+ countries. 3 billion+ children immunized against polio

Our Guiding Principles

The Four-Way Test

A moral code for personal and business relationships, used by Rotarians worldwide since 1932.

I

Is it the TRUTH?

Of the things we think, say, or do — we ask first whether it is true.

II

Is it FAIR to all concerned?

We consider whether our actions are fair to everyone affected.

III

Will it build GOODWILL and better friendships?

We strive to create goodwill and strengthen relationships.

IV

Will it be BENEFICIAL to all concerned?

We ensure our work benefits everyone, not just a few.

Areas of Focus

What We Work On

Rotary's seven areas of focus address the world's most pressing needs.

🕊️

Promoting Peace

Building understanding and mediating conflicts through Rotary Peace Centers.

👶

Maternal & Child Health

Supporting safe mothers, safe births, and healthy children worldwide.

💉

Fighting Disease

Leading the fight against polio and expanding access to healthcare.

📚

Supporting Education

Providing literacy programs, scholarships, and Teacher Exchange programs.

🌍

Protecting the Environment

Sustainable water projects, tree planting, and environmental conservation.

💧

Providing Clean Water

Building wells, water filtration systems, and sanitation infrastructure.

🌾

Growing Local Economies

Microfinance, vocational training, and entrepreneurship development.

🏥

Polio Eradication

Since 1988, helping immunize 3 billion+ children. Polio cases reduced by 99.9%.

🤝

Community Service

Local projects that make a difference right here in Alamosa and the SLV.
