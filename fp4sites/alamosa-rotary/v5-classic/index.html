About Rotary

Rotary was founded on February 23, 1905, when Paul Harris — a young attorney blown into Chicago on the first transcontinental railroad — sat down with three fellow businessmen and formed a club based on fellowship and service.

That small gathering grew into the world's oldest and largest service club. Today, 1.2 million Rotarians in more than 200 countries work together to address the most pressing needs in their communities and around the globe.

The Alamosa Rotary Club is a diverse group of professional leaders committed to Service Above Self — a motto that guides everything we do, from local projects in the San Luis Valley to global initiatives that save lives.

Rotary at a Glance

  • Founded 1905 by Paul Harris, Chicago
  • 1.2M+ members across 200+ countries
  • 47M volunteer hours per year
  • $273M in global grants (2024-25)
  • 3B+ children immunized against polio
  • 99.9% reduction in polio cases
  • Rotary Peace Fellows at 9 universities
  • The Four-Way Test as our moral code

The Four-Way Test

A moral code for personal and business relationships, introduced in 1932 and used by Rotarians worldwide.

First

Is it the TRUTH?

Of the things we think, say, or do

Second

Is it FAIR to all?

Is it fair to all concerned?

Third

Will it build GOODWILL?

Will it build goodwill and better friendships?

Fourth

Will it be BENEFICIAL?

Will it be beneficial to all concerned?
