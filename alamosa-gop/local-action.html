Alamosa County

Map of Alamosa County — Precincts & Districts

Alamosa County sits in the heart of the San Luis Valley at the southern end of the Colorado Rockies. Population approximately 6,300.

Board of County Commissioners

The Board of County Commissioners is the governing body of Alamosa County, comprised of three members elected to four-year staggered terms. The Board typically meets on the second and last Wednesday of each month.

A comment card is required to speak during public comment periods. Commissioners hear and determine all matters requiring action by the Board.

The City of Alamosa

City of Alamosa — Election Wards Map (2023)

Alamosa went from a tent city to a rail center in June 1878 and was incorporated on August 30, 1878. Today the city has a population of approximately 9,000 and serves as the hub of the San Luis Valley.
